FC Barcelona

ABOUT

FC Barcelona, Spanish professional football (soccer) club located in Barcelona. FC Barcelona is renowned for its historically skillful and attractive brand of attacking football that places an emphasis on flowing, open play. The team is part of a wider sports and social club with thousands of members.

Founded in 1899 by a group of Swiss, Catalan, German, and English footballers led by Joan Gamper, the club has become a symbol of Catalan culture and Catalanism, hence the motto "Més que un club" ("More than a club"). Unlike many other football clubs, the supporters own and operate Barcelona. It is the third-most valuable football club in the world, worth $5.6 billion, and the world's fourth richest football club in terms of revenue, with an annual turnover of €800.1 million. The official Barcelona anthem is the "Cant del Barça", written by Jaume Picas and Josep Maria Espinàs. Barcelona traditionally play in dark shades of blue and garnet stripes, hence nicknamed Blaugrana.

ANTHEM

Throughout its history, the club has had various official songs. The anthem in use today is "Cant del Barça" (The Song of Barça), composed in 1974 on the occasion of the club's 75th anniversary. Authors Josep Maria Espinàs and Jaume Picas composed the lyrics in Catalan, while the music was composed by Manuel Valls The song was first performed on 27 November 1974 at Camp Nou before the match between FC Barcelona and the East Germany national team by a 3,500-man choir led by Oriol Martorell.

On November 28, 1988, in celebration of the club's centenary, the song was performed by Catalan singer-songwriter Joan Manuel Serrat at the end of the festival at Camp Nou. Since the 2008-09 season, el Cant del Barça has been featured on the official Barcelona jerseys.

El Cant del Barça is turned on before Barcelona games take place at Camp Nou, especially during matches against Real Madrid and just before the start of the meeting. The song is also often played for supporters and fans to cheer, chant and celebrate the victory.

Championships

MESSI

Lionel Messi's tenure at FC Barcelona is nothing short of legendary. Joining the club's youth academy, La Masia, at the age of 13, Messi quickly rose through the ranks to become one of the most prolific and influential players in football history. Over his 21-year career at Barcelona, Messi scored an astonishing 672 goals in 778 appearances, making him the club's all-time top scorer1. His incredible goal-scoring record includes 10 La Liga titles, 4 Champions League trophies, and 7 Copa del Rey titles. Messi's ability to perform under pressure, his vision on the field, and his unparalleled dribbling skills have earned him numerous individual accolades, including 8 Ballon d'Or awards3. His partnership with teammates like Xavi and Iniesta created some of the most memorable moments in football history, solidifying his status as a Barcelona icon and one of the greatest players of all time

PEP GUARDIOLA

Pep Guardiola's tenure at FC Barcelona is often regarded as one of the most successful periods in the club's history. Taking over as manager in 2008, Guardiola led Barcelona to an unprecedented treble in his first season, winning La Liga, the Copa del Rey, and the UEFA Champions League. Over his four-year tenure, he guided the team to 14 trophies, including two Champions League titles, three La Liga titles, and two Spanish Super Cups1. His innovative tactics and emphasis on possession-based football revolutionized modern football and solidified Barcelona's reputation as a dominant force in world football. Guardiola's Barça is remembered for its beautiful, attacking style of play, characterized by the mesmerizing performances of players like Lionel Messi, Xavi Hernandez, and Andres Iniesta. His success at Barcelona laid the foundation for his future managerial stints at Bayern Munich and Manchester City, where he continued to achieve remarkable success
